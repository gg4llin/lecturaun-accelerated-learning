## 3. VOICE CHARACTERISTICS

### What We Sound Like:
- **Direct.** No fluff. No corporate speak. Every word earns its place.
- **Human.** We write like a smart friend who happens to know a lot about education.
- **Energetic.** We're excited about what we're building and it shows.
- **Specific.** We name names. "Lolita Vasquez Ramon" beats "our Spanish AI teacher" every time.
- **Honest.** We acknowledge we're growing. We don't pretend to be something we're not yet.

### What We Don't Sound Like:
- ❌ Corporate ed-tech: *"Our proprietary adaptive learning algorithm optimizes student outcomes."*
- ❌ Condescending: *"Even parents with no teaching experience can use Lecturaun!"*
- ❌ Vague: *"A better way to learn."*
- ❌ Desperate: *"SIGN UP TODAY — LIMITED TIME OFFER!!!"*
- ❌ Beige: Any voice that could belong to any other company.

## 6. VOCABULARY GUIDE

### Use These Words:
- **Accelerated** (not "advanced" — accelerated implies speed AND quality)
- **Mastery** (not "completion" — we care about actual understanding)
- **Teacher** (not "AI model" or "bot" — they are teachers)
- **Earn** (achievements are earned, not given)
- **Verifiable** (blockchain credentials are verifiable, not just "digital")
- **Parent/Teacher** (acknowledge that homeschool parents ARE teachers)
- **Child/Student** (both are acceptable; vary by context)

### Avoid These:
- ❌ "Revolutionary AI" (vague tech-hype)
- ❌ "Cutting-edge" (cliché)
- ❌ "Personalized learning journey" (overused ed-tech speak)
- ❌ "Robust platform" (corporate nothing-word)
- ❌ "Seamless experience" (meaningless)
- ❌ Any language that diminishes teachers, parents, or students

## 7. THE NFT/BLOCKCHAIN CREDENTIAL ANGLE

This is a differentiator that requires careful handling.

**What it is:** Every achievement a student earns is hashed on-chain — a permanent, verifiable proof of learning that belongs to the student forever.

**How to talk about it:**
- Lead with the **meaning**, not the technology: *"Proof that can't be taken away."*
- Acknowledge it's early: *"We're building the credential system of the future. Today, your child is getting in first."*
- Never lead with "NFT" to non-crypto audiences — lead with "verifiable credential" or "permanent achievement"
- To crypto-native audiences, the NFT angle IS the hook — lead with it

**What NOT to say:**
- ❌ "Mint your child's achievements as NFTs!" (alienates non-crypto parents)
- ❌ "Blockchain-powered learning!" (tech-speak over substance)
